About

• What is ADR?

Adverse Drug Reaction (ADR) can be described as an injury caused due to administration of any medication. ADRs may occur as a result of administration of a single dose of medication or due to prolonged drug usage patterns.

Also, an ADR may be initiated as a result of therapy including a combination of drugs.


• What is the importance of an ADR reporting system?

An ADR reporting system enables timely detection of ADRs, following which adequate measures for controlling the damage caused and preventing any further damage can be taken by healthcare professionals in order to provide the highest quality of healthcare facilities to all individuals.

Moreover certain reaactions can be plotted as a pattern after administration of a particular medication. Monitoring and reporting the same enables an elimination of the root cause of ADRs.

Reporting and monitoring of ADRs also provides a platform for research and development of finest quality of medication. The data so obtained is also utilised for educational purposes.


• Why is this software developed?

The ADR reporting system ensures accurate and timely reporting of ADRs. It also ensures security and privacy of sensitive patient information. Moreover, the system facilitates reduction of data redundancy and data loss due to illegible text while also decreasing human effort involved in the reporting process.


• How does this system work?

The system provides a simple form strcuture on a website which can be accessed by any healthcare professional. The form provides simple input fields, radio buttons, checkboxes, dropdown selectors, date pickers and various other such elements which are easy-to-use and familiar from their use in other web portals/applications etc. Once the data entry process is complete, the system generates a PDF file which can be downloaded and sent to the concerned authorities for further processing.


• Advantages of using this system

 • No data entered by user is stored by the system, ensuring complete data security.

 • No data entered by the user is sent to the server.

 • Any given user would not be able to access the data being entered by any other user.


• Where does the data go?

The data after validation, processing and editing (if required) is sent to our local adverse drug reaction monitoring center (AMC) for further escalation to national authorities acting under the Pharmacovigilance Program of India (PvPI).
